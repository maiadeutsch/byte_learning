1. Under the Hood

Now that we understand tags, here is the basic breakdown of any HTML page:

The entire page will be contained between an opening and a closing <html> tag.

The first section after the opening html tag is called the 'head' of the webpage

2. The Body!

The section underneath the 'head' is called the 'body'.

Any content that's placed in the body will appear in the webpage.

Any content that's placed in the head of the webpage won't appear.

3. Back to The Head!

The head is where you place all of the non-content.

This includes things like the author and title of the webpage.

This also includes any CSS and Javascript that you place in the head.

4. Spicing Up Our Head

Try putting <title>My First Webpage</title> in the head section.

Nothing will happen to the content of the webpage...

But the browser will now display the title of the webpage at the top of the page. Wow.

5. Working the Body

Now that we understand the head, let's enhance the body.

Enclose the Hello, World! in your webpage with a <b> tag.

It should look like this: <b>Hello, World!</b>

Your text will now be bold.

6. Working the Body

The <i> tag makes text italicized, and <u> makes text underlined.

Now try writing multiple lines of text.

There's going to be a problem: HTML doesn't recognize line-breaks.

With HTML, to signify a new line you use the <br /> tag.

7. Whitespace

The <br /> tag is unique in that it doesn't have a closing tag.

It's one of the few tags that just stands on its own.

However, sometimes it's more convenient to use the <p> tag.

8. Paragraphs

The paragraph tag needs to be closed with a </p> closing paragraph tag.

The end of a paragraph will always have two line-breaks after it.

All of the lines in this page are included in separate paragraph tags.
