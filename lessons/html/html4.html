1. Links and Images

Okay, let's spice things up with links and images.

Let's start by adding a link. Links are made with the <i> tag, the anchor tag.

Copy this into your body: <a href="http://www.google.com">This is a link to Google!</a>

Now you've added a link to the Google homepage to your website.

2. Attributes Galore

However, the anchor tag is different from our previous tags.

It's the first tag we've seen with attributes!

The attribute here is called 'href', and its value is the URL that our link will take us to.

3. More on Attributes

Many tags have attributes. Even the body tag can have added attributes.

For example replace your <body> tag with <body bgcolor="red">

These days we use CSS for things involving color.

Next, let's add an image to our site

4. Adding Images

Cute, no? Copy the following code into your body section:

<img src="images/bunny.jpg" height="100" width="100" alt="Cute Bunny!" title="Cute Bunny!" />

5. Where's the Image?

Notice how it doesn't quite do what you want it to do?

That's because the source, or 'src', is pointing to an image in the 'images' folder called 'bunny.jpg'.

But that image doesn't exist on your computer!

6. Adding Your Image

Go to the folder where you have your .html file.

Create a new folder there called 'images'.

Drag an image file into the new 'images' folder.

7. Ah, There's My Image!

Look again at this code:

<img src="images/bunny.jpg" height="100" width="100" alt="Cute Bunny!" title="Cute Bunny!" />

Replace 'images/bunny.jpg' with the name of your own image.

Now your image should work!

8. More on Images

There are a few more things to note in our image code:

<img src="images/bunny.jpg" height="100" width="100" alt="Cute Bunny!" title="Cute Bunny!" />

Our code specifies the height and width of the image.

Otherwise, the image would just have its original dimensions.

The dimensions here are in pixels, little squares of color on your screen.

9. Alt and Title

Secondly, you'll notice that we have attributes called 'alt' and 'title'.

That's the text that appears when you hover your mouse over the image.

Some browsers make the 'alt' text appear, others browsers make the 'title' text appear.

It's always safer to include both.

10. Image Files

And finally, you'll notice that the image is a jpg, or jpeg.

The three most image file types you'll find on the internet are:

JPEG (.jpg), GIF (.gif), and PNG (.png)

These image files types are small and portable

You don't want your webpage to load slowly because of large files!
